Always-on Inspiration

Hand-picked ideas for right now.

Charcoal Drawing
Draw your non-dominant hand holding something small
Place a coin, key, or pebble in your weaker hand and draw it from observation. Focus on the gesture of the fingers, not the object itself.
⌛ 20 minutes◈ Beginner
Watercolour
Paint the view from your window right now
Whatever is outside — grey sky, brick wall, or garden — paint it with loose wet-on-wet washes. The constraint of subject matter is the entire challenge.
⌛ 30 minutes◈ Any level
Photography
Shoot 10 images without moving your feet
Stand in one spot and make 10 completely different photographs. Zoom with your body — crouch, stretch, turn. Forces creative problem solving.
⌛ 15 minutes◈ Any level
Ink Drawing
Draw a familiar room from memory, then compare
Draw your kitchen or bedroom from memory first. Then sit in the room and draw it again. Compare the two — the gaps reveal your visual assumptions.
⌛ 45 minutes◈ Intermediate
Clay Sculpture
Sculpt an emotion, not a thing
Choose a feeling — relief, anticipation, grief — and make a palm-sized abstract form that embodies it. No figures, no objects, just shape and texture.
⌛ 1 hour◈ Any level
Oil Painting
Limited palette still life: 3 colours only
Set up 3 objects and paint them using only yellow ochre, ultramarine blue, and white. Mix every other colour yourself. Teaches you more about colour than any other exercise.
⌛ 2 hours◈ Intermediate
